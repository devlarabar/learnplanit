The Protégé Effect

Learning by teaching, often referred to as the "protégé effect," is a powerful educational approach with several key benefits. When individuals teach what they've learned to others, it reinforces their own understanding and retention of the material. It encourages deeper comprehension, critical thinking, and the ability to explain complex concepts in simpler terms.

Additionally, it boosts confidence and communication skills as learners become more proficient in conveying information. This method fosters a sense of responsibility and accountability, promoting active engagement with the subject matter. Ultimately, learning by teaching not only enhances one's own knowledge but also encourages a collaborative and interactive approach to education, benefiting both the teacher and the learner in the process.
